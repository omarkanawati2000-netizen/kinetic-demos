Got Questions?

Frequently Asked
Questions.

How quickly can you respond to a plumbing emergency?
+

For emergency calls within Colorado Springs, our average response time is under 60 minutes. We have technicians on-call 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year — including holidays. When you call, you'll speak to a real person (not a machine) who can dispatch immediately.

Do you provide free estimates?
+

Yes — we provide free written estimates for all non-emergency work. For emergency service calls, there is a standard dispatch fee which is waived when you proceed with the repair. We always give you the full price upfront before any work begins, so there are never any surprises on your invoice.

What should I do if I have a burst pipe?
+

First, locate your main water shut-off valve and turn it off immediately to stop the flow of water. This is usually near the water meter or where the main line enters your home. Then call us at (719) 555-1234. While you wait, open faucets to drain remaining water from the pipes and move valuables away from the affected area if safe to do so. Do NOT use electrical appliances near standing water.

Are you licensed and insured in Colorado?
+

Absolutely. Joe's Plumbing holds a valid Colorado Master Plumber license and is fully bonded and insured. We carry general liability insurance as well as workers' compensation coverage for all employees. We pull all required permits for permitted work, ensuring your project is code-compliant and won't cause issues when you sell your home.

How do I know if I need a water heater repair or full replacement?
+

Generally, if your water heater is under 10 years old and the issue is a failed heating element, thermostat, or pressure valve, a repair makes sense. If it's over 10–12 years old, or if there's significant sediment buildup, corrosion, or tank leaking, replacement is almost always the smarter long-term investment. We'll give you an honest recommendation either way — we don't push replacements when a repair will do the job.
