Colorado's semi-arid climate and altitude (6,000+ ft) make plant selection critical. We love native and drought-tolerant plants like Blue Grama Grass, Rocky Mountain Penstemon, Salvia, Rabbitbrush, Catmint, and Buffalo Grass. These beauties thrive with minimal water once established, resist our unpredictable late spring frosts, and look stunning year-round. We'll help you choose plants that are both beautiful and sustainable for your specific microclimate.
In Colorado Springs, we typically recommend spring cleanups in late April to mid-May, once the last frost danger has passed (usually after Mother's Day). Irrigation system startups happen around the same time. Book early — our spring slots fill up fast, usually by March. Fall winterization and cleanup should be scheduled in September–October before the first hard freeze. Call us now to get on the schedule!
